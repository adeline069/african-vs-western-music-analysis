
#  A Data Analysis of Streaming Performance Across Music Genres

Analyzing how audio features influence streaming popularity 
across Afrobeats and Western music genres.

## Project Overview
This project uses Spotify audio feature data to compare Afrobeats with selected Western genres.
The goal is to examine whether differences in musical characteristics ,such as danceability, energy, valence, and tempo , 
help explain variations in streaming popularity.
As someone who grew up listening to both Afrobeat and Western music,
I wanted to let the data tell the story.

## Research Question

**Danceability & Popularity**

Does higher danceability in Afrobeat translate into increased streaming popularity compared to western genres?

**Valence**

Do higher valence levels in Afrobeats correlate with increased streaming popularity?

**Tempo & Danceability**

How does Afrobeats tempo compare to Western genres and how does tempo influence danceability?

**Underrepresentation**

Is Afrobeats underrepresented in streaming popularity relative to its audio characteristics compared to western genres?

**Energy& Marketing Insight**

Does higher energy lead to increased popularity and what does this imply for music marketing strategies?


## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle – Spotify Tracks Dataset
- 6000 songs across 6 genres (1,000 per genre)

## Genres Analyzed
 African: Afrobeat  
 Western: Hip-Hop, R&B, Blues, Jazz, Soul

## Key Findings
- Higher danceability does not guarantee streaming popularity , Hip-Hop leads in both, but Afrobeat ranks 2nd in danceability yet only 4th in popularity

- Afrobeat demonstrates the strongest emotional positivity (valence) among all genres, indicating strong potential for global audience appeal

- Energy has virtually no correlation with popularity (r = 0.0011), suggesting that musical intensity alone does not drive streaming success

- All genres share similar tempo ranges (110–125 BPM), indicating that tempo is not a differentiating factor between African and Western music

- A consistent gap exists between Afrobeat's strong audio characteristics and its streaming popularity, pointing to the role of external factors such as platform exposure and promotion
